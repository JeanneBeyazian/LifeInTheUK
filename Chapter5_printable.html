Chapter 5: UK Geography

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the countries of the UK and their capitals
  • Recognize major geographical features including mountains, rivers, and islands
  • Understand population distribution and major urban areas
  • Learn about UK Overseas Territories and Crown Dependencies

1. The United Kingdom: Basic Facts

The United Kingdom (UK) is made up of four countries: England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. The full name is "The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland."

Basic Geographic Facts

  • Location: Northwestern Europe
  • Total land area: 243,610 square kilometers (94,060 square miles)
  • Total UK population: About 67 million
  • Highest point: Ben Nevis, Scotland (1,345 meters/4,413 feet)
  • Longest river: River Severn (354 kilometers/220 miles)
  • Largest lake: Lough Neagh, Northern Ireland
  • The UK is surrounded by:
    • North Sea (east)
    • English Channel (south, separating UK from France)
    • Irish Sea (west, separating Great Britain from Ireland)
    • Atlantic Ocean (northwest)

2. Physical Geography

Great Britain and Northern Ireland

  • Great Britain: The largest island containing England, Scotland, and Wales
  • Northern Ireland: Located on the island of Ireland, sharing a border with the Republic of Ireland

Major Mountain Ranges

  • England:
    • Pennines (backbone of England, running from Derbyshire to Scotland)
    • Lake District (northwest, including Scafell Pike, England's highest mountain)
    • Cheviot Hills (forming part of the England-Scotland border)
  • Scotland:
    • Highlands (northern Scotland)
    • Grampian Mountains (including Ben Nevis, UK's highest mountain)
    • Southern Uplands (southern Scotland)
  • Wales:
    • Snowdonia (northern Wales, including Snowdon, Wales' highest mountain)
    • Cambrian Mountains (central Wales)
    • Brecon Beacons (southern Wales)
  • Northern Ireland:
    • Mourne Mountains (southeast, including Slieve Donard, Northern Ireland's highest mountain)
    • Sperrin Mountains (northwest)

Major Rivers

  • England:
    • Thames (flows through London to the North Sea)
    • Severn (longest river in the UK, flows from Wales through western England)
    • Mersey (northwest, flows through Liverpool)
    • Tyne (northeast, flows through Newcastle)
    • Trent (central England)
  • Scotland:
    • Clyde (flows through Glasgow)
    • Tay (longest river in Scotland)
    • Forth (flows past Edinburgh)
    • Tweed (forms part of the England-Scotland border)
  • Wales:
    • Wye (forms part of the England-Wales border)
    • Taff (flows through Cardiff)
    • Dee (northeast Wales)
  • Northern Ireland:
    • Bann (longest river in Northern Ireland)
    • Lagan (flows through Belfast)
    • Foyle (flows through Londonderry/Derry)

Notable Geographic Features

  • England:
    • White Cliffs of Dover (southeast coast)
    • Lake District (national park with many lakes)
    • Yorkshire Dales (northern England)
    • Cotswolds (picturesque hills in central England)
  • Scotland:
    • Loch Ness (large, deep loch famous for the legendary monster)
    • Loch Lomond (largest lake by surface area in Great Britain)
    • Scottish Highlands (mountainous region)
    • Hebrides, Orkney, and Shetland Islands (archipelagos)
  • Wales:
    • Snowdonia National Park
    • Pembrokeshire Coast (southwestern coastline)
    • Gower Peninsula (first Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty in the UK)
  • Northern Ireland:
    • Giant's Causeway (unique rock formation on the north coast)
    • Lough Neagh (largest lake in the British Isles)
    • Causeway Coast

Climate

  • Temperate maritime climate
  • Mild winters and cool summers
  • Rainfall throughout the year, more in western areas

3. Major Urban Areas

England's Major Cities

  • London:
    • Capital of England and the UK
    • Population of about 9 million in Greater London
    • Located in southeast England on the River Thames
    • Major global financial center
    • Home to the UK government and monarchy
  • Birmingham:
    • Second largest city in the UK
    • Located in the West Midlands
    • Industrial heritage and manufacturing center
  • Manchester:
    • Located in the northwest
    • Major industrial city, particularly known for cotton industry
    • Now a center for media, culture, and sports
  • Liverpool:
    • Located in the northwest on the River Mersey
    • Historic port city
    • Cultural center, birthplace of The Beatles
  • Leeds:
    • Located in West Yorkshire
    • Financial and cultural hub of West Yorkshire
  • Newcastle upon Tyne:
    • Located in the northeast on the River Tyne
    • Industrial heritage in shipbuilding and coal
  • Bristol:
    • Located in the southwest on the River Avon
    • Historic maritime city

Scotland's Major Cities

  • Edinburgh:
    • Capital of Scotland
    • Located in the southeast on the Firth of Forth
    • Historic city with Edinburgh Castle
    • Home to the Scottish Parliament
    • Famous for the Edinburgh Festival
  • Glasgow:
    • Largest city in Scotland
    • Located in the west on the River Clyde
    • Industrial heritage and shipbuilding
    • Cultural and commercial center
  • Aberdeen:
    • Located in the northeast
    • Known as the "Granite City"
    • Center for North Sea oil industry

Wales's Major Cities

  • Cardiff:
    • Capital of Wales
    • Located in the south on the Bristol Channel
    • Home to the Welsh Parliament (Senedd)
    • Industrial heritage in coal export
  • Swansea:
    • Second largest city in Wales
    • Coastal city in the southwest
  • Newport:
    • Located in southeast Wales
    • Industrial city with historic port

Northern Ireland's Major Cities

  • Belfast:
    • Capital of Northern Ireland
    • Located on the east coast at the mouth of the River Lagan
    • Historic shipbuilding industry (Titanic was built here)
    • Home to the Northern Ireland Assembly at Stormont
  • Londonderry/Derry:
    • Second largest city in Northern Ireland
    • Located in the northwest
    • Historic walled city

4. Administrative Divisions

England

  • Divided into nine regions:
    • North East
    • North West
    • Yorkshire and the Humber
    • East Midlands
    • West Midlands
    • East of England
    • London
    • South East
    • South West
  • Within these regions are counties, metropolitan counties, and unitary authorities
  • Historic counties include Yorkshire, Lancashire, Kent, Sussex, etc.

Scotland

  • Divided into 32 council areas for local government
  • Historic regions include:
    • Highlands
    • Lowlands
    • Borders
    • Grampian
    • Strathclyde

Wales

  • Divided into 22 unitary authorities
  • Historic counties include Glamorgan, Gwynedd, Dyfed, etc.

Northern Ireland

  • Divided into 11 districts for local government
  • Historic counties:
    • Antrim
    • Armagh
    • Down
    • Fermanagh
    • Londonderry/Derry
    • Tyrone

5. Islands and Coast

Major Islands and Archipelagos

  • Scotland:
    • Inner Hebrides (including Skye, Mull, Islay)
    • Outer Hebrides (including Lewis and Harris)
    • Orkney Islands (north of the Scottish mainland)
    • Shetland Islands (furthest north, closer to Norway than London)
  • England:
    • Isle of Wight (south coast)
    • Isles of Scilly (southwest)
  • Wales:
    • Anglesey (northwest)
  • Northern Ireland:
    • Rathlin Island (north coast)

Coastline

  • The UK has approximately 12,500 kilometers (7,700 miles) of coastline
  • No point in the UK is more than 125 kilometers (75 miles) from the sea
  • Notable coastal features:
    • White Cliffs of Dover (Kent)
    • Jurassic Coast (Dorset and East Devon)
    • Giant's Causeway (Northern Ireland)
    • Gower Peninsula (Wales)
    • Cornwall's rugged coastline
    • Scottish sea lochs and fjords

6. Population and Demographics

Population Distribution

  • Total UK population: approximately 67 million
  • England is the most densely populated country (about 56 million people)
  • Scotland has the lowest population density
  • About 83% of the UK population lives in urban areas
  • Highest population densities are in:
    • Greater London
    • West Midlands (around Birmingham)
    • Greater Manchester
    • West Yorkshire (around Leeds)
    • Central Belt of Scotland (between Edinburgh and Glasgow)
  • Lower population densities in:
    • Scottish Highlands
    • Central Wales
    • Northern England
    • Southwest England

Demographic Characteristics

  • Aging population with median age around 40
  • Multicultural society with diverse ethnic backgrounds
  • Major religious groups: Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, Sikhism, Judaism, Buddhism
  • English is the main language, with Welsh, Gaelic, and Scots also having official status in their respective regions

7. UK Overseas Territories and Crown Dependencies

Crown Dependencies

  • Not part of the UK but self-governing possessions of the Crown
  • Isle of Man: Located in the Irish Sea between Great Britain and Ireland
  • Channel Islands:
    • Jersey
    • Guernsey (including smaller islands of Alderney, Sark, and Herm)
  • Have their own legislative assemblies and legal systems
  • The UK is responsible for defense and international relations

UK Overseas Territories

  • 14 territories under UK sovereignty but not part of the UK itself - most have their own governments but remain under UK sovereignty. The UK is responsible for defense and foreign affairs.
  • Notable territories include:
    • Gibraltar: Located at the southern tip of Spain. Particular historical and strategic importance to the UK.
    • Falkland Islands: South Atlantic islands
    • Bermuda: North Atlantic island
    • British Virgin Islands: Caribbean islands
    • Cayman Islands: Caribbean islands
    • Turks and Caicos Islands: Caribbean islands
    • British Antarctic Territory
